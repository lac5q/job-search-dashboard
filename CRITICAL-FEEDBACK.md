## Hard Truths (Critical but Helpful)

### 1. 60-90 Days is TIGHT But Doable
**Reality Check**:
- Median time to offer in 2025: 68.5 days
- You're targeting the average, which means you need to be ABOVE average in execution
- Contract roles move faster than full-time (good news)
- You need to start TODAY, not tomorrow

**What This Means**:
- You can't waste time perfecting things
- Good enough and shipped > perfect and delayed
- You need volume AND quality
- You must treat this like a full-time job (or at least 20-30 hours/week)

### 2. Your "Superpowers with Cursor" Might Be a Trap
**The Risk**:
- Over-automating your job search can make you look like a bot
- Personalization wins in 2025 - recipients can smell mass outreach
- Tools can help with tracking, NOT with relationship building

**The Right Way to Use Automation**:
- ✅ Track contacts and follow-ups
- ✅ Aggregate job postings
- ✅ Schedule social media posts
- ❌ Auto-send connection requests (risky)
- ❌ Auto-send messages (kills personalization)
- ❌ Scrape emails and blast (spam)

**Better Approach**:
- Use automation for FINDING opportunities
- Use manual effort for CONNECTING with people
- Save templates, but customize each one 30-50%

### 3. "Networking" Without a Strategy = Wasted Time
**Common Mistake**:
- Random coffee chats with no clear ask
- Connecting with everyone on LinkedIn
- Asking "do you know anyone hiring?" (too vague)

**Better Approach**:
- Target people who can ACTUALLY hire you or refer you
- Be specific: "I'm looking for contract work in email marketing automation for e-commerce brands. Do you know anyone at [specific company] or [specific agency]?"
- Offer value first: "I noticed your emails could be optimized - happy to share some quick ideas"

**Action**:

### 5. Contract Work Requires Different Positioning
**What You Need to Emphasize**:

## Where You'll Likely Fail (And How to Avoid It)

### Failure Point 1: Inconsistency
**The Pattern**:
- Week 1: Super motivated, do everything
- Week 2: Burn out, do nothing
- Week 3: Panic, try to catch up
- Week 4: Give up

**The Fix**:
- Sustainable pace: 3-4 hours daily, NOT 8 hours for 3 days
- Daily non-negotiables: 5 outreach, 1 application, 10 min LinkedIn
- Rest on Sundays, review on Saturdays

### Failure Point 2: Spray and Pray
**The Pattern**:
- Apply to 100 jobs with same resume
- Send same message to 50 people
- No follow-ups
- No tracking

**The Fix**:
- Quality > Quantity
- 10 targeted applications > 50 random ones
- Always follow up after 5-7 days
- Track everything in spreadsheet

### Failure Point 3: Waiting for Perfect
**The Pattern**:
- Spend 3 weeks building perfect portfolio
- Rewrite resume 20 times
- Don't reach out until "ready"

**The Fix**:
- Ship version 1.0 TODAY
- Improve while doing
- "Good enough" is better than "not done"

### Failure Point 4: Ignoring Rejection
**The Reality**:
- 90% of outreach will be ignored
- 80% of applications won't respond
- You'll get rejected A LOT

**The Fix**:
- Expect rejection as the norm
- Track your %, not raw numbers
- 10% response rate is GOOD
- Keep going, it's a numbers game

### Failure Point 5: Not Following Up
**The Miss**:
- 80% of sales happen after 5+ touchpoints
- Most people give up after 1 message

**The Fix**:
- Always follow up after 5-7 days
- 2nd follow up after another week
- Then move on
- Use tracking sheet to manage this
